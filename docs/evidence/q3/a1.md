# Q3 — Cost Model + Unit Economics

## Assumptions

The assumptions stated below apply for all three scale points and are based on the described use case related to ESADE career services.

Every MAU sends an average of 20 messages per day during 5 active days per month, resulting in 100 messages per MAU per month. Each message contains an average of 3,000 input tokens including an approximately 500-token system prompt, three Qdrant RAG chunks at 500 tokens each, and about 500 tokens of conversation history. There are 400 output tokens generated per message. Each message involves one embedding call of approximately 200 tokens via OpenRouter. For every five messages there is a file upload averaging 2 MB, giving 40 MB of object storage per MAU per month. CloudWatch log ingestion is assumed at 0.5 GB per month at 10 MAU scaling linearly. Retention periods for chat history and uploads are 90 days and 1 year respectively.

For LLMs we selected DeepSeek V4 Flash at $0.14 per million input tokens and $0.28 per million output tokens. We chose this model because it supports the OpenAI API requiring no infrastructure changes to integrate with LobeChat, and its cost is approximately 80% lower than AWS Bedrock Claude Haiku at comparable performance. Embeddings use OpenRouter text-embedding-3-small at $0.02 per million tokens. All prices are AWS eu-west-1 list prices from June 2026.

## Top 3 Cost Drivers at 1000 MAU

**1. Fixed infrastructure: 68% of total cost**

Both EC2 instances and RDS Multi-AZ cost $364.99 per month regardless of usage. At 1000 MAU this is 68% of the total bill. At 10 MAU it rises to 83%. This is the defining characteristic of a self-hosted platform: steep fixed costs with very low marginal cost per additional user.

**2. CloudWatch logs: growing linearly with users**

At 1000 MAU, CloudWatch log ingestion costs $25 per month and scales directly with usage. At 5,000 MAU it would reach $125 per month. This is controllable through log sampling and shorter retention policies but easy to overlook during initial planning.

**3. LLM token costs: model choice matters significantly**

At 1000 MAU, DeepSeek V4 Flash costs $53.20 per month combined. The same workload using AWS Bedrock Claude Haiku would cost approximately $420 per month, and GPT-4o would exceed $2,000 per month. The choice of DeepSeek is therefore not just a technical preference but the single most important cost decision in the architecture, keeping token costs under 10% of the total bill even at scale.

## Cost-Cutting Levers

**Lever 1: Reserved Instances for EC2 and RDS --> estimated 35% savings on compute**

The two EC2 instances and RDS cost $364.99 per month on-demand. Purchasing 1-year reserved instances reduces this to approximately $237 per month, saving $128 per month or 23% of the total bill at 1000 MAU. The trade-off is a 12-month financial commitment with no refund if the deployment is decommissioned early. The SLO impact is zero.

**Lever 2: Stop the primary Qdrant EC2 instance during off-hours --> estimated 20% savings**

The r6i.large Qdrant instance costs $106.43 per month running continuously. Stopping it outside business hours using an AWS Lambda scheduled trigger reduces runtime to approximately 13 hours per day, cutting the Qdrant compute cost to $63.85 per month and saving $42.58. Switching RDS to single-AZ saves a further $60 per month, giving a combined saving of approximately $102 per month or 19% of the total bill. The trade-off is significant: Qdrant being offline overnight means semantic search is completely unavailable outside business hours, breaking the RAG CV feedback use case for any user active at those times. RDS single-AZ also increases RTO from under 2 minutes to 20 to 40 minutes on failure.

## Unit Economics

| Scale | Monthly Total | Cost per MAU |
|---|---|---|
| 10 MAU | $437.90 | $43.79 |
| 100 MAU | $448.13 | $4.48 |
| 1000 MAU | $547.30 | $0.55 |

The mathematical break-even against ChatGPT Enterprise at $30 per user per month is approximately 15 MAU, calculated as the fixed monthly infrastructure cost of $437 divided by the $30 gap between SaaS pricing and our near-zero variable cost per user. However the operational burden is only clearly justified when the cost advantage is substantial. At 30 to 50 MAU the cost per user is $9 to $15, a 50 to 70% advantage over SaaS, which is where self-hosting becomes strategically defensible.

Past 100 MAU the economics become overwhelming. At $4.48 per user we are 85% cheaper than the least expensive comparable SaaS alternative.

## Pricing Recommendation

At 100 MAU the total platform cost is $448.13 per month. To achieve a 50% gross margin, the required monthly revenue is cost divided by 0.50, giving $896.26 per month or $8.97 per user. Rounding to $9 per user per month achieves a 50.2% gross margin, covers the AWS bill with buffer for support and maintenance overhead, and remains significantly below any competing SaaS product. At 1000 MAU the same $9 price generates $9,000 per month against a $547 cost, a margin of 94%, funding infrastructure scaling and product development with substantial headroom.

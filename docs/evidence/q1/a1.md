# Q1 — Use case design

## Context

The ESADE Business School is a graduate private university in Barcelona, consisting of 2,500 students studying in MBA, Master, and Executive Education programs. The Career Services department comprises 8 professional counselors that help students during CV writing, prepare for interviews and provide contacts within companies.

However, nowadays Career Services face serious limitations related to the current organizational structure. Each counselor works with 300+ students every academic year. A CV check requires about 25-40 minutes on average. Students often ask simple questions like 'What's the deadline for applying?', 'Who will prefer my CV?' or 'Are there any specific requirements for industry X?'. That means that students have to wait from 5 to 10 business days for advice while valuable coaching time is spent on low-complexity tasks.

In addition to that, Career Services accumulates a significant amount of knowledge. Alumni job history, various feedback reports from employers, and anonymous examples of successfully used CVs remain in common drives and Notion databases. Using the LobeChat stack on AWS EC2, eu-west-1, students will have an opportunity to get answers from the institution's AI chatbot anytime 24/7 without transferring any source documents to third parties.


## Personas

**Sofia, second-year MBA student**

She is applying for consultancy firms, and requires help with her CV, at 11 p.m., but since career services will be shut at that time, Sofia has no choice but to send her email today. Though she has a copy of her CV, she doesn’t know how to customize her CV for McKinsey. Sofia sends her request for advice to her advisor, who asks her to upload her CV within one week.

**Marc, Career Services Advisor**

Marc dedicates around 60% of his workweek replying to questions that he has had to answer multiple times. Marc gets to devote more time to complex training programs like case interview training since the assistant handles all the trivial questions without any need for assistance. Additionally, he leverages the assistant to extract notes on the employer through Notion prior to a presentation.

**Elena, Director of Career Services**

Elena at present does the manual collation of data on placements from the spreadsheets. She can now use the virtual assistant to find out which groups of students are the least involved in using the career services and get the results in the form of an automated report based on usage records that cannot be linked to individual identities. Typically, Elena uses the virtual assistant to correlate data on applications and placements.


## Journey

The following describes Sofia's interaction on the night before her McKinsey application deadline.

**Step 1: Authentication**

Sofia logs in to LobeChat through Casdoor SSO authentication, which supports OAuth 2.0 authorization code flow. Casdoor authenticates Sofia based on the lobechat organization and generates a JWT with a lifetime of seven days before redirecting her to the chat window. The session is encrypted with KEY_VAULTS_SECRET and signed with NEXT_AUTH_SECRET, which are kept secure on the EC2 instance.

**Step 2: Document Upload**

Sofia uploads her CV as a PDF. The file is stored in the lobe bucket on MinIO, exposed via its own subdomain with a Let's Encrypt TLS certificate managed by Caddy. MinIO runs on a dedicated subdomain rather than a path prefix specifically to avoid SigV4 signature mismatches that occur when S3 requests are rewritten by a reverse proxy. The CV remains on the EC2 instance's EBS volume and is never sent to an external service directly.

**Step 3: RAG Query via Qdrant**

Sofia writes: "Please review my CV for a McKinsey interview and compare it with the successful applicants." Sofia's question is embedded as a vector with the help of the OpenRouter text-embedding-3-small model. Embedding entails sending her question outside the EC2 instance first to OpenRouter and then to the servers of OpenAI. That's an architectural compromise as complete data sovereignty requires running embedding locally with, say, nomic-embed-text through Ollama as the solution to avoid this outside interaction entirely. Next, the qdrant-mcp tool, one of 11 MCP servers managed by MCPHub, searches for the most semantically similar chunks within the career-knowledge-base dataset in Qdrant and pulls out top-three matches consisting of anonymous CVs of successful applicants as well as McKinsey recruiters' remarks.

**Step 4: Document Analysis**

LobeChat fetches the CV directly from MinIO based on the object reference stored and parses the textual information. DeepSeek Chat uses the CV text as well as the chunks retrieved from Qdrant as inputs for context and provides an answer with the relevant weaknesses and their corresponding rewording options based on historical preferences of McKinsey recruiters. The final prompt containing the retrieved chunks is sent to DeepSeek's API, although the source documents and knowledge bases remain local.

**Step 5: Notion Knowledge Retrieval**

Sofia wonders, “What is the application deadline for McKinsey and who is the ESADE recruiter?” The notion-mcp command, which is authorized through Bearer authentication in OPENAPI_MCP_HEADERS, searches the Career Services Notion workspace for the structure employer record, which contains deadline, recruiter contact name, and the notes from last year’s campus visit. Marc is never logged on.

**Step 6: Streaming Response**

The response is sent back token by token using Server-Sent Events. The setting of Caddy flush_interval -1 guarantees that the response is flushed immediately, allowing Sofia to see the formation of the response on the fly rather than having to wait until its completion. On average, the whole process takes 8 seconds.
On the other hand, the underlying database is updated by Career Services advisors through periodic ingestion of new applications when each recruiting period ends. Each file is divided into chunks, processed with embeddings, and added to the career-knowledge-base collection of Qdrant retrieval layer, thus maintaining it without any tech skills of the team members.


## Why this stack vs ChatGPT/Copilot

**Raw documents and source data stay inside the EU**

ChatGPT Enterprise and Microsoft Copilot utilize server infrastructure that does not assure EU-only residency without costly dedicated agreements. In this setup, all raw resumes, comments from employers, and KB documents will remain exclusively on the EC2 machine in eu-west-1. While the embedding phase involves querying the text on OpenRouter, the prompt built from selected pieces gets sent to DeepSeek outside the system, but the original documents do not get transferred anywhere. This difference between keeping the raw data internally versus querying text externally makes a significant operational difference for organizations managing students' career-related data under the GDPR regime. Adopting a locally executed embedding model would resolve the last external call issue completely.

**The proprietary knowledge base cannot be shared with a vendor**

This is because the CV samples, notes from recruiters, and briefing notes by employers that give value to this use case are ESADE's competitive advantage. Feeding such data to ChatGPT or Copilot will mean moving it to the server owned by OpenAI and Microsoft, where it might end up being used for training future models. The use case in question ensures that the entire knowledge base is housed in Qdrant using the EC2 instance.

**MCP tools enable capabilities SaaS products cannot match**

There is no native integration between ChatGPT/Copilot and our own customized Notion workspace, no capability to perform queries on vector embeddings specific to our institutions, nor any access to the file system. MCPHub has been designed to connect 11 different MCP instances, one of which is called notion-mcp which can run queries based on live workspace data, and another called qdrant-mcp for semantic search on ESADE data using ESADE's own vector embeddings. Our assistant can leverage employer data in real time from Notion alongside historical data from Qdrant in one turn.

**Cost at institutional scale**

At 2,500 users, the ChatGPT Enterprise model, which is priced at about 30-60 dollars per month per user, will come to approximately 75,000-150,000 dollars per month, depending on the scale of the contract. The current implementation stack uses only one t3.xlarge instance of the EC2 service, whose pricing is roughly 130 dollars per month. Despite 50 messages per month from every user, each averaging 500 tokens in volume, DeepSeek's rate of 0.27 dollars per million input tokens adds another roughly 17 dollars per month in costs for tokens. Total cost for 2,500 users is under 200 dollars per month.
